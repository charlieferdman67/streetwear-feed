StreetwearPulse

@streetwearpulse

This project explores the influence of social media on contemporary street fashion through a deep analysis of datasets, imagery, Instagram activity, and other digital platforms. Rather than taking a surface‑level approach, we conducted extensive searches to uncover meaningful patterns linking online fashion engagement with real‑world self‑expression.

The data reveals clear correlations: where digital engagement is high, street style is more visible, diverse, and expressive — especially in major fashion capitals. To visualise the relationship, we distilled our findings into three graphs connecting virtual buzz to on‑the‑ground trends.

From Feeds to Foot‑Traffic — Key Takeaways

  1. The internet’s not a mirror, it’s a megaphone. Cities loud online usually buzz in real life; hashtags and reels are today’s cultural supply chain.
  2. Distance matters way less now. Seoul and Hong Kong may be far from Paris, but constant posts and a strong local scene keep them in play.
  3. Hype alone isn’t enough. Budapest proves it — likes don’t open stores. Real‑world infrastructure and daily wear count most.
  4. The old hubs still lead (but not by much). New York, London, and Paris have built‑in advantages, yet digital‑first cities are closing fast.

Changes for Next Time — What I’d Tackle Next

  • Time series: Track month‑to‑month hype spikes from drops or collabs.
  • TikTok data: Shorter videos, bigger reach — does that change which cities stand out?
  • Interviews: Chat with local shop owners in “quiet‑but‑loud” cities like Budapest to find out what’s blocking real‑world growth.

The big picture: Streetwear spreads at Wi‑Fi speed, but it still needs real streets to make it real.
